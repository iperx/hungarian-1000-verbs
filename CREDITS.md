# Credits, sources, and licenses

## How it was made (short)

1. **Verb selection and frequency order.** A Hungarian word-frequency list derived from
   OpenSubtitles subtitles was lemmatized and POS-tagged with HuSpaCy (`hu_core_news_md`)
   to pull out verbs and rank them by frequency. The top 1000 were curated (canonical
   head, infinitive, gloss, register).
2. **Example sentences and translations.** For each verb, one short, lifelike A1-A2
   sentence was generated with a large language model (Anthropic Claude), kept to a
   pronoun subject and the correct verb pattern (vonzat, the case the verb takes). The Russian and
   English translations were produced from the Hungarian.
3. **Validation.** Every sentence passed a structural check (the target verb is actually
   present, single sentence, length) and several independent LLM review passes with
   different lenses (Hungarian grammar, naturalness, and verb usage; translation accuracy
   and naturalness). Debatable grammar and verb patterns were cross-checked by hand against
   dictionaries. Around a dozen sentences were corrected; the rest converged clean.
4. **Audio.** Each sentence was synthesized with two Hungarian neural voices.

This is an AI-assisted resource, validated through review and dictionary checks rather
than written by a professional lexicographer. It is meant for everyday A1-B1 study, so
please report anything that looks off.

## Sources

- **Frequency data:** OpenSubtitles (a Hungarian subtitle corpus), via the OPUS project.
  https://opus.nlpl.eu/OpenSubtitles and https://www.opensubtitles.org
- **Lemmatization and POS tagging:** HuSpaCy, model `hu_core_news_md`.
  https://github.com/huspacy/huspacy (Apache-2.0)
- **Dictionaries** (grammar and verb-pattern checks): English Wiktionary
  (https://en.wiktionary.org) and Wikiszótár.hu (https://wikiszotar.hu).
- **Generation and QA:** Anthropic Claude (a large language model).
